Skincare: Acne Treatments

Self

12/13/20

Acne is annoying and almost everyone I know has struggled with it at some point in their life. It can be frustrating and confusing to treat, due to the abundance of skincare information there is on the internet. I have personally struggled with acne for many years and continue to struggle with it. Because my skin is not perfect, I have watched many many YouTube videos and tested many different acne treatments myself. Before diving into acne treatment products, it’s important to understand the basics of skincare. Check out our skincare basics article here if you haven’t already!

DISCLAIMER: I am not a dermatologist and I am purely speaking about skincare and skincare products from my own personal experience. If you have skin concerns, please consult a dermatologist or esthetician.*

There are many different kinds of acne treatments on the market. Some need to be prescribed, some don’t. Some are cheap and some are expensive. Some are extremely gentle, and others could have intense side effects such as drying of the skin or purging. With all skincare, it’s incredibly important to listen to your skin and be gentle. Be gentle with how you interact with your skin and be gentle with the products you apply.

Today, I will be covering a few acne-treatment ingredients: AHAs, BHAs, and Retinoids. Here are some important precautions to take with acne treatments and skincare in general:

  • Always start out slow when introducing a new treatment product. You can do this by:
    • using your new product once a week
    • using moisturizer as a buffer (apply moisturizer first, then your treatment)
    • combine your treatment with your moisturizer
  • Make sure to apply acne treatment products on dry skin (after you cleanse). Damp skin may cause your acne treatment to penetrate deeper into the skin, increasing your chances of skin irritation.
  • Make sure to apply sunscreen daily! Treatments can leave the skin more prone to sun damage. Even if you do not use treatments, using a sunscreen every day is beneficial for anti-aging and skin cancer prevention.
  • Treatments take time. Give your products a chance to work! Check out Mixed Makeup’s video here for detailed information on how long it should take for your products to work. Typically, you should give your products at least 2-3 months to show improvement.
  • Be cautious of over-using your treatment and potentially receiving a bad reaction from a new product. Everyone’s skin is unique so it is important to pay attention to how your skin reacts. Patch testing is a good way to gauge how your skin reacts to new products. To patch test, apply the product to a small area on your skin for a few days/applications.[1]

Now let’s jump into some treatment ingredients! Chemical exfoliants such as AHAs and BHAs help to exfoliate dead skin cells on the surface of the skin. They help reveal fresh, new skin and can help with acne and texture.[2]

Alpha Hydroxy Acids (AHAs)

Alpha hydroxy acids or AHAs are chemical exfoliants that help to exfoliate dead skin cells on the surface of the skin. Between AHAs and BHAs, AHAs are gentler and not oil-soluble. AHAs work more towards the surface of the skin than BHAs; they do not penetrate as deeply as BHAs. Some AHAs include glycolic acid, mandelic acid, and lactic acid. Retinoids

Retinoids are a common ingredient used to combat hyperpigmentation, skin texture, acne, and anti-aging! Some over-the-counter retinoids which you can buy without a prescription include retinol, retinaldehyde, retinyl esters, and granactive retinoids. Over-the-counter retinoids go through a conversion process that converts them into retinoic acid. This conversion process is what makes over-the-counter retinoids gentler than prescription strengths retinoids such as tretinoin. Nonetheless, all retinoid types can be powerful and effective in combatting acne issues. For more information on retinoids, watch Mixed Makeup’s video on the different kinds here. Ok, now I know about these different kinds of acne treatments. Which one do I choose?
I recommend starting out with an AHA or BHA product. If your skin is very sensitive, I would say try the Krave AHA chemical exfoliant. Otherwise, feel free to try out an AHA or BHA product. The most important advice I can give is to not over-do it! When I first started using a BHA exfoliant, I made the mistake of using it every single night. This actually made my skin worse and much more irritated. If you have already tried AHA/BHA products and want to try something else, retinol is a great option for you. With all treatments, I personally believe it is much more beneficial to stick with 1 product rather than to use a bunch of different products and not know what is actually helping or hurting your skin.

Alternative Option: Skincare Reset

If you have felt like you have tried everything possible (I felt this), making a skincare reset might be ur best option. Bring it back to the basics: cleanse, moisturize, apply SPF. Do this for a few weeks or even months and see how your skin feels. Use this time to heal your skin with hydrating, rich ingredients. This helped me a lot after over-using chemical exfoliants!

Good luck and remember that skincare is a journey! Looking forward to your skin glow-up. :D
