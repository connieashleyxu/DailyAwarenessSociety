Skincare is extremely important, whether it’s for treating acne, preventing anti-aging, or just protecting yourself from the sun and the potential of developing skin cancer. In this article, we will be discussing the skincare basics: the 3 main skin types, the importance of having a skincare routine, and our recommendations to help you get started.* (We will discuss our experiences with tackling specific skincare concerns such as acne and hyperpigmentation in future articles.)

The 3 main types of skin are oily, combination, and dry.[2] [3] Note that you can be a combination of these skin types; for example you can be normal to oily. Your skin can also be drier during the colder months and more oily during the hotter months.

Oily skin is characterized by:

  • shiny face
  • enlarged pores
  • large number of blackheads and whiteheads

Combination skin is characterized by:

  • oily T-zone (area above eyebrows and down on your nose)
  • dry skin on cheeks and other areas

Dry skin is characterized by:

  • flaking skin
  • patchy, rough areas

Before we dive into what a simple skincare routine consists of, here are some important notes to keep in mind:

**As a disclaimer, we are not doctors and are speaking solely from our personal experience as people with oily and combination skin. In some areas of this article, we will clarify which skin type we are speaking from.**

POV: Oily Skin

Something that I have learned through my experiences with treating acne is that it is so so so important to maintain a healthy skin barrier. Your skin barrier is the first defense to all external threats and helps maintain the delicate balance of your body. [4] If you are using a chemical exfoliator every single day, that can make it super easy for your skin barrier to become compromised. Remember that your skin is intelligent and the purpose of your skin is to serve as the first line of defense. If you are constantly stripping your skin of its natural oils, your skin will not be able to properly do its job and you may find yourself with sensitive, red, and even more acne-prone skin. Even though I have oily skin, I still prefer hydrating cleansers and hydrating treatment formulas for my acne.

POV: Combination Skin

Identifying which type of skin you have is crucial to choosing products so that you can best complement and avoid irritating your skin. One mistake that I made when I was younger was not choosing the correct products for my skin. I thought that I had oily skin, when in reality I had combination skin. I used harsh products that would suck all the oil out of my face and even in places where my face wasn’t oily. This made my face extremely dry. If only I had learned sooner what skin type I have!

It is essential that you develop a skincare routine to keep your skin healthy and protected from damaging UV rays. Your routine does not have to be elaborate or expensive. It can be simple and personalized for you.

Here is a simple skincare routine for beginners to follow! We will also be giving you recommendations for each step including affordable products that are easy to buy online!

A general skincare routine consists of an oil-based cleanser, water-based cleanser, moisturizer, and sunscreen. Oil-based cleansers are good for removing makeup, sunscreen, and oils on the skin in a hydrating, non-stripping manner. We recommend using an oil-based cleanser because just doing a single-cleanse with a water-based cleanser might not effectively remove all your makeup or sunscreen (**from chemistry class, “like dissolves like” - if you don’t use an oil-based cleanser, the excess oils on your skin won’t be cleaned off). You should follow up with a water-based cleanser to cleanse the skin of any residue from the oil-based cleanser. After cleansing, use a moisturizer to hydrate the skin and seal in moisture. This is important for a healthy, strong skin barrier. Finally, protect your skin with sunscreen. We have listed some recommendations below.
