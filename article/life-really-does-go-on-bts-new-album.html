Life Really Does Go On. BTS’ New Album.

Arts & Entertainment

11/22/20

You may have heard of the “the biggest boy band in the world” or, as they’ve recently been named, Wall Street Journal’s 2020 Music Innovator: BTS.[1]

[BTS on the cover of Wall Street Journal for the special Innovators Issue.]

BTS holds the “Guinness World Record for staging the biggest virtually attended livestream music performance, which attracted fans from over 100 countries."[2] They’ve also often been referred to as “the Beatles of the 21st century."[3]

But what makes BTS so special is their connection with their fans, named A.R.M.Y. (which stands for Adorable Representative MC for Youth), through the topics that their music is about. Their songs have covered topics from bullying, mental health, and even topics that are taboo within the South Korean government.[4]

Their ability to relate and connect with their fans has continued through their most recent comeback with their new album “BE” and music video “Life Goes On.” What makes this album and comeback even more special is the BTS members’ direct involvement in the production, design, and direction of the music. As their label has said, “This album is made more special by direct involvement from BTS in not only the music, but from the concept and composition to design. You will discover throughout the album the thoughts, emotions and deepest reflections of BTS while experiencing an even richer musical spectrum”.[5] The music video was even directed by Jeon Jung-Kook, BTS’ youngest member.

“Life Goes On” is the title track for their new album, touching on the fact that “‘even in the face of this new normality, our life goes on’ and imparts a message of healing to fans and to the world.”[6]

“One day, the world stopped
Without any warning
Spring didn't know to wait
Showed up not even a minute late”
“Like an echo in the forest
The day will come back around
As if nothing happened
Yeah, life goes on
Like an arrow in the blue sky”

These are just some of the lyrics that directly address how they’ve been able to connect and relate to the hardships that everyone has faced during the COVID-19 pandemic.

[Scene from “Life Goes On” music video. BTS filmed their music video in their own house in order to depict the reality of what the majority of the world faces.]

The official “Life Goes On” music video “earned a preliminary 68 million YouTube views in its first 24 hours,” making it “one of the top 5 biggest debuts ever” on Youtube.[7] Despite this, as of 11:52 AM CST, November 22, “Life Goes On” has only had 61 total spins on US pop radio as journalist Brian Patrick Byrne has reported.[8]

This is not the first time that BTS has faced discriminiation in the Western media scene. Their music video views are always deleted by Youtube and are never given the proper recognition at American award shows, with the creation of separate categories such as “K-pop."[9][10] This comes despite the fact that white Canadian singers and white British boy-groups can be nominated for main awards at American award shows, but a Korean boy group is moved into a separate category.

Really, despite all this, BTS has continued to stay positive and optimistic, serving as the young generation’s role model by partnering with UNICEF on the ‘Love Myself’ campaign, starting in 2017. This global UNICEF campaign “aimed at the protection of young people so they can live without the fear of violence, and promotes the hashtag – #BTSLoveMyself, which asks fans and supporters to post self-loving photos with the hashtag to different social media platforms”.[11] The proceeds of this campaign are “used to support young victims of school violence, domestic violence and sexual assault around the world, as well as education programs for local communities to aid violence prevention."[12] This campaign has raised approximately $2.8 million USD as of October 2020.[13]

Not only has BTS collaborated with UNICEF, they have spoken at the 73rd and 75th United Nations General Assembly in 2018 and 2020, respectively. Their most recent appearance at the 75th United Nations this past September gave them the platform to speak on loving oneself and supporting one another during this hard time, previewing their new song “Life Goes On” by ending the speech with “Life goes on. Let’s live on."[14][15]
