Opinion: Apple Event

Industry

10/18/20

Recap

Apple held its annual iPhone event on Tuesday, October 13. They released 4 new iPhones including the iPhone 12, iPhone 12 mini, iPhone 12 Pro, and iPhone 12 Pro Max. They also released the HomePod mini, an addition to the HomePod family.

iPhone 12 Family

The iPhone 12 serves as an update to the previous iPhone 11. The iPhone 12 Pro and iPhone 12 Pro Max serve as updates to the previous iPhone 11 Pro and iPhone 11 Pro Max. The iPhone 12 mini is a new addition to the iPhone family, with its 5.4-inch screen. All new iPhone 12 models feature a new design similar to that of the current iPad Pro and iPad Air models. The new iPhones have a more squared design and come in all-new colors.

The key changes for all new iPhone 12 models include support for 5G, improved displays, support for MagSafe accessories, new chips, better cameras, and supposedly more drop-proof bodies with Apple and Corning’s new “Ceramic Shield” material.

The pro models include the additional telephoto lens for zooming into photos without losing image quality, night mode portraits, and “HDR video recording with Dolby Vision up to 60 fps” compared to the non-pro iPhone 12’s “HDR video recording with Dolby Vision up to 30 fps”. The pro models also have a LiDAR (Light Detection And Ranging) scanner to enable faster autofocus in darker lighting environments when taking photos and AR (augmented reality) applications.

HomePod Mini

The new HomePod mini serves as a cheaper alternative to the original HomePod, coming in at $99. Apple spoke about its 360-degree audio and a feature where you can take a song you are listening to on your iPhone and transfer the audio to the HomePod by placing your phone next to it. The HomePod mini is also capable of detecting different users based on voice, making it personalized towards the user asking Siri questions.

Opinions

Opinion 1

I found the Apple Event to be underwhelming. The upgrades to the iPhone family were pretty minor in my opinion. 5G is currently not widely available and is also battery-consuming. According to industry trade group GSMA, around half of the mobile connections will be 5G by 2025[1]. With this in mind, I would say that it’s not worth buying a new iPhone 12 model solely because of 5G.

Other upgrades such as the processor and cameras are not incredibly selling points to the average consumer. I found the MagSafe accessories to be pretty interesting and exciting, but these accessories are also not bundled with your iPhone 12 if you decide to buy one; this means Apple has created another area for you to spend your money in, alongside a new case and screen protector.

The HomePod mini didn’t really excite me or disappoint me. It’s a typical smart speaker, just smaller. If you loved the original HomePod, you’ll love this one as well.

Opinion 2

I am an avid Apple user. All of my products are Apple, which allows me to sync all of my data and information easily. Because of this, it is very hard for me to switch to a Samsung phone or a Google pixel. Their technology is so much more advanced and customizable compared to Apple.

I was definitely hoping for more upgraded features such as a 120 Hz refresh rate on the screens, but I think Apple only stuck to upgrading one feature, which was just 5G capabilities.

I am also a little disappointed because the notch on the iPhone is still there and has not shrunk either. In comparison to Samsung phones, the notch has disappeared and the front camera takes up just a circle of space on the front screen. I was expecting there to be more upgrades, but the only major change I can see is the shape of the iPhone has changed to a more “boxy” look.

Our iPhone Recommendation

If you have a fully functional iPhone X or newer model, there really is no need to upgrade to the new iPhone 12 models. Overall, there have not been many new innovations that customers will see huge differences with.

If you are planning on upgrading your iPhone but are not sure which model (iPhone 12 mini, iPhone 12, iPhone 12 Pro, or iPhone 12 Pro Max) you want, here are our recommendations. If you want a super large phone, the iPhone 12 Pro Max is basically your only choice (for the average person’s hand, it is extremely large and is very hard to use one hand to type). If you want a flagship iPhone that is not too large, the iPhone 12 is your choice. We recommend the 12 over the 12 Pro because the differences in terms of features are not massive and the 12 costs $200 less. We recommend getting the iPhone 12 Pro over the iPhone 12 if you find the telephoto lens to be important to have or you are a super passionate/professional iPhone photographer who wants the capabilities of Apple’s ProRAW image format.

For a more in-depth comparison of iPhone devices, you can go to https://www.apple.com/iphone/compare/.
