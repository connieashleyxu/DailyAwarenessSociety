Air Travel Safety During a Pandemic

Health

01/03/21

Q: I have to travel via plane and I’m super nervous because of COVID-19. What can I do to travel as safely as possible while on a budget?*

Have any other questions or topics you want to see us cover? Feel free to reach out to us via email at dailyawarenesssociety@gmail.com or via DM @dailyawarenesssociety on Instagram!

If you absolutely have to travel during this time, consider traveling via car if the trip is able to be made without stopping at a hotel.[1] However, this may not be feasible for everyone. So, if flying by plane is absolutely necessary, here are some tips to travel as safely as possible:

  1. A plane actually has ventilation features that makes it quite safe to travel. Peterson from NBC explains it quite well:[2]
    • Fresh air continuously enters both engines at -65 degrees. Temperature and pressure are increased, then air is passed through a control valve and cooled by additional outside air.
    • HEPA filters remove 99.7 percent of particles; new technology could destroy 100 percent of all bacteria and viruses.
    • Filtered, recirculated cabin air and fresh air are combined.
    • The aircraft is divided into ventilation segments of three to seven rows; you share air only with passengers in your segment.
    • Outflow valve continuously releases cabin air and helps maintain constant pressurization of aircraft.
    [3] Image provided by MIT that explains the ventilation features on a plane.
  2. Choosing the safest seat. Currently, more major airlines are flying at full capacity (meaning, they will be trying to fill every seat on the plane). The aisle seats may be your go-to for more legroom pre-pandemic, but these are actually one of the worst seats during the pandemic! During the pandemic, the window seats are the best seats to take. If your budget allows it, try getting a first-class window seat. First-class seats are more spaced out, which is always a plus! [4] Diagram provided by National Geographic that explains the likelihood of COVID-19 infection on a plane.
  3. What to wear. Here is a list of clothing items you should consider wearing at the airport.
    • N-95 mask or Elastomeric Respirator. The typical surgical mask or cloth masks will not prevent inhaling the virus -- only prevent spreading the virus to others. Both the N-95 masks and Elastomeric Respirators prevent inhaling the virus. However, the N-95 masks are low in production, so they should be saved for frontline workers who come into contact with COVID patients on nearly a daily basis. The Elastomeric Respirator does the trick as well to prevent inhaling the virus. [5]
    • Safety Goggles. To prevent contact of the airborne disease from getting into your body via your eyes, wear safety goggles.
    • Face Shield. As an extra layer of protection, wear a face shield to prevent large virus particles from coming into contact with your face.
    • Gloves. You will inevitably need to touch a lot of things at the airport, to prevent direct contact with your skin, wear gloves that are easily removed after your tenure at the airport.
    • Wear layers with long sleeves and long pants (bring an empty trash bag in your carry on). With layers, as soon as you are at your destination, you can remove clothes that came into contact with the outside world and place them into a trash bag for 3 days. After 3 days, any remaining virus on your clothes should be mostly gone.
  4. What to bring. Here’s a list of items to have on hand when traveling.
    • Hand Sanitizer. Even when wearing gloves, make sure to apply hand sanitizer regularly after touching anything!
    • Alcohol Wipes. Before sitting down (on a waiting seat at the airport or in your airplane seat), wipe down your vicinity (the seat, the armrest, the seat belt, the window area, the table tray, your luggage, your passport, etc.). After getting out of the airport, sanitize your luggage and other belongings.
    • Straw. When traveling you want to refrain from drinking and eating. But, if you must drink water, bring a straw so that you don’t have to remove your mask. Simply feed the straw through the bottom of your mask to take a sip of water.
    • Waterbottle. Most airlines are still providing complimentary drinks and snacks, so you can simply pour the complimentary drink into your water bottle and drink using the straw method mentioned above.
  5. Refrain from eating, drinking, and using the restroom. This may be easier said than done, especially if you have a long flight. Try to eat a large meal before you head into the airport and use the bathroom as well! You should be able to go for the duration of the trip without eating. But, drinking and using the restroom may be more difficult to avoid.

    To drink water, try using the straw method mentioned in tip 4.

    To use the restroom, be sure to sanitize anything you will come into contact with at the restroom (toilet, faucet, etc.) and try bringing a couple of sheets of your own toilet paper.
  6. Turn on the fan in the airplane and point it downwards. Turning on the fan will help push some virus particles to the ground and the airplane ventilation should help with the rest![5]

There is also news of COVID-19 vaccines coming out, so the hopes are we can begin returning to our life pre-COVID as best as we can soon! But, until then, be sure to stay safe. Safe traveling!
