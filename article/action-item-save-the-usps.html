Action Item: Save the USPS

Politics

08/23/20

With every election season comes a new set of conflicts and challenges, and the 2020 election season is no exception. While the U.S. is still battling the novel Coronavirus pandemic, the political polarization between liberals and conservatives has only been rising.

On Wednesday, August 12th, President Trump revealed that he was going to block needed funding to the United States Postal Service (USPS). But, how does this impact the voting season?

With COVID-19, heading to in-person polls can be dangerous for high-risk voters. While some states have already made the needed accommodations to make voting registration, mail-in voting, and in-person voting safer during this pandemic, there are still a large number of states that are not prepared for the November election time during a pandemic.[1] One of the safest ways of voting during a pandemic is offering the option for mail-in voting. However, in order to accommodate the larger influx of voting ballots by mail, funding would need to be allocated towards the U.S. Postal Service.

Looping back around to August 12th, Trump told Fox Business Network that he will be “holding up funding” to the USPS.[2] Later that week, Trump admitted that he did not choose to provide USPS the funding it needed to make it harder for mail-in ballots to be processed because he claims that mail-in ballots lead to increased voter fraud.[3]

However, there is research to show that election fraud is rare in mail-in voting.[4] In other words, there should be no fear of this election season being “the most INACCURATE & FRAUDULENT Election” (as Trump tweeted on July 30) because of mail-in ballots.[5] Moreover, mail-in ballots do not favor one political party over another, and both liberals and conservatives will be able to vote in the safest way possible amidst a rampaging pandemic.

There are a couple of key facts to take from Trump’s recent actions.

First, in order to make democracy in the U.S. as fair as it can be, it is crucial that everyone who is eligible to vote is able to do so. States like California, Washington, and Colorado are making efforts to ensure the safety of its voters throughout the entire process (from registering to voting, whether it may be via mail or in-person). Therefore, it is completely possible for other states to follow suit and ensure safe alternatives for voters to allow them to fulfill their civic duty without fear of contracting COVID-19. With stunted funding for the USPS, states can no longer ensure the mail-in option for their voters, therefore censoring an entire group of potential voters that could change the election results.

The second problem lies in the fact that the USPS employs “more than 97,000 military veterans” and is one of the leading postal services in the U.S.[6] In these unprecedented circumstances, there are so many workers fighting to keep an income to live. With USPS losing needed funding, the jobs of millions are at risk. Given that we are in an election season and we are living through a pandemic, everybody needs the USPS. The Trump Administration needs to be providing funding to the USPS which would in turn help millions of Americans with not only their right to vote, but also some with their livelihoods.

Currently, there are two bills that could drastically save the USPS.

1) H.R.2382 - USPS Fairness Act. “This bill repeals the requirement that the U.S. Postal Service annually prepay future retirement health benefits.”

2) H.R.6800 - The Heroes Act. “This bill responds to the COVID-19 (i.e., coronavirus disease 2019) outbreak and its impact on the economy, public health, state and local governments, individuals, and businesses.”

We need the USPS to ensure our votes can be counted properly. The change to make sure we have a better future coming out of this election season starts with you.
