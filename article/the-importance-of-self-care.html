The Importance of Self-Care

Self

08/09/20

The past four to five months have been quite isolating and difficult. Having to stay at home almost every day has been challenging. Towards the beginning of this social-distancing period, I struggled to establish a daily routine and to keep up with my own mental and physical health. However, I’ve been able to use this time to learn how to take better care of myself.

With school coming up soon, it’s no doubt that my peers and I will experience a steep increase in workload and stress. Taking care of yourself is incredibly important to reduce these stress levels and stay healthy. Too much stress can lead to more serious issues in the long run, including sleep dysfunction, heart disease, depression, etc.[1]

To prevent these kinds of issues from arising we should implement daily acts of self-care into our routines.* In fact, practicing self-care has been shown to improve cognitive ability, including memory and focus. Making time for yourself gives you the chance to recharge and relax.[2] I am no expert, but here are the main ways that I have been practicing self-care during this quarantine:

Exercise

Staying physically active is something that I have come to appreciate during this quarantine. Maintaining an almost daily exercise routine gave me a sense of normality and helped me feel more energized. I was recently introduced to @rrayyme’s Instagram account where she records live IGTV workouts. I enjoyed these workouts because of the verbal coaching and the variety of exercises offered. My favorite kind of workout she does from her Instagram is the “54 Card Pickup” series in which every card in a deck is attributed to a specific exercise. Red cards are more cardio-focused whereas black cards are more strength-focused. I recently discovered another YouTuber, Sydney Cummings, who also has tons of effective HIIT workouts and videos targeting different areas of the body.

Before this quarantine, I did not work out very frequently, and even when I did, it wasn’t structured or frequent. One method that helped me stay on track is working out with a partner or a group of friends. Scheduling a time to hop on Zoom and workout together is the perfect way to hold you and others accountable.

TV

At the end of the day or whenever I am in need of a break, I enjoy going onto Netflix or YouTube to unwind. It’s a great way to take your mind off of things and even educate yourself in an entertaining way. Here are some of the latest shows, movies, and YouTube channels I have been watching:

  • Pose (Drama) - This show does an incredible job of displaying the emotional depth of the LGBTQ community as well as displaying people in the community as “normal people with normal problems”[3]. I also appreciate how the show cast queer people to play queer roles.
  • Avatar: The Last Airbender (Animation)
  • It’s Okay to Not Be Okay (K-drama)
  • The Gift (Thriller)
  • Fractured (Thriller)
  • I Am Mother(Sci-fi/Thriller)
  • Beauty Within on YouTube - Recently I’ve been loving watching skincare videos on YouTube since it’s so relaxing. If you’re looking for your next cleanser or moisturizer, look no further!

Reading

Reading is a great way to relax and plunge into a new world; it’s a chance to escape reality. Aside from the excitement and intrigue that reading a book can bring, it’s actually also an effective way to reduce stress. According to a 2009 study by the University of Sussex, reading for six minutes reduces tension levels by up to 68%![4] If you enjoy thrillers, I highly recommend The Woman in the Window by A. J. Finn; I was absolutely hooked and was shocked by the end.

Sleep

Another fantastic way to practice self-care is to sleep. Naps can be a great way to quickly recharge during the day. But besides naps, it’s also important to keep a regular sleeping schedule. If you can, try to go to sleep a little earlier than usual. You may find yourself waking up earlier and feeling more productive about your day. However, there is also nothing wrong with allowing yourself to sleep in when you need that extra boost of rest.

I came across an article by Matt Ragland that detailed how going to bed earlier changed his life. Maintaining a healthy and happy lifestyle truly is a cycle. For him, exercising caused him to eat better and also become tired around bedtime.[5] I have also found that regular exercise and pushing to improve myself in regard to my physical health has increased the quality of my sleep.

There are many more ways that you can practice self-care, whether it’s continuing your passion or experimenting with various side projects. At the end of the day, these are just suggestions for ways to practice self-care. Self-care is not black and white and there is no one way to do it.
