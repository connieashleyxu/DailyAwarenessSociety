Back to School: Productivity

Productivity

09/06/20

My summer break quarantine was very relaxing and chill. I watched a lot of TV, had many FaceTime calls, and built Minecraft houses with my friends. But now that college has started, I had to quickly do a full 180 and figure out how to get back in the school grind. This seemed pretty difficult, especially after having a seemingly 6-month-long break. However, the transition hasn’t been too bad so far.

In this article, I will be providing notes on key ways that I have kept myself productive and apps that I have found incredibly helpful with this process. Even though productivity is a great feeling and something we strive for throughout our school and work life, it’s important to also tackle the issues that can arise with feeling unproductive. We’ve definitely all had moments in which we felt guilty for having fun or taking rest. I’ve realized that many societal pressures can cause these feelings and that we don’t need to feel this way if we just reshape our views on what productivity truly means.

One important difference to understand is that productivity and being busy do not mean the same thing. As author Hannah Braime said on a post on Becoming Who You Are:

“[W]e want to detach from busy-ness for the sake of being busy. An activity or pursuit is only productive if it’s actually adding something to our lives.”

She also detailed the need to change how we look at rest. Taking moments to rest, have fun, or recharge help to create balance in life. Another great point that Braime makes is that self-worth needs to be changed from a condition to a value[1].

“[D]eepening our sense of self-worth becomes easier when we hold self-worth as a value, not as the result of something conditional.”
“The things we do as a result of valuing our self-worth might be similar, but the energy behind them is quite different. It’s avoiding pain (in this case, the pain of not feeling worthy) versus seeking growth (starting from a place of having self-worth as a key value).”

Now that we have clarified what productivity really means, here are some key changes that I have integrated into my daily life to keep myself on track in school and in life:

1. Clean Working Environment

I revamped both my working environment and my bedroom since, together, they were going to be my “dorm” for the next half year. It is important to create a workspace and living space that you feel happy in. I installed a new desk, got a new monitor, and a succulent for some added greenery. In my bedroom, I did some decorating by ordering some photos and printing some posters off of FedEx. Especially during a time like now where we are staying at home much more, I recommend making your living space look and feel however you want to. Now is the perfect time to spend some time creating an environment that you enjoy living and working in.

Once you have created your own personal workspace, I recommend keeping it clean and tidy. Having a busy school schedule can be stressful enough, so I have found that making sure I go to sleep with my workspace ready for the next day is helpful with setting a fresh, productive mood for the morning.

2. Compartmentalization

One way that I’ve been keeping myself focused on my work is by compartmentalizing my work life and personal life within my house. Normally, I would be doing that by calling my dorm my personal space and the libraries around campus my working space. However, given the current circumstances and the fact that I am staying home this semester, I have dedicated one room for work and one room for relaxation.

To keep yourself focused, you could also put your phone in a different room and turn on “Do Not Disturb” on your devices to prevent yourself from getting distracted when you want to get work done.

3. Give Yourself Breaks

Another key component of productivity is non-productivity, well to a certain extent :P. It’s important to give yourself small breaks here and there so that you don’t burn out or become overwhelmed. You could take a walk, go work out, call a friend, etc. Feel free to check out our article on self-care and Instagram post for more things to do during your small breaks throughout the day!


Now that we have covered some fundamental principles to becoming and staying productive, here are some apps that I have found incredibly useful for planning and keeping track of everything in my life:

Notion - iOS, Android, Mac & Windows

This is an app that I discovered shortly before college started and wow it made me excited. Notion is an app that you can use for planning, making to-do lists, note-taking, journaling, and more. The great thing about Notion is that there is so much that you can customize and people all over the internet have designed templates that you can use based on your needs. I have been using Notion for so many aspects of my life. Here are some of the pages that I have made so far:

  • Weekly Plan - I go here every day to schedule what tasks I need to complete for the day. I also created a template button so that I can easily prepare for a new week of todos.
  • Master Schedule and Class Pages (template)
    • Master Schedule - This is where I store all of the tasks that I have. Once I received my syllabi, I put all of my homework, quizzes, and exams into this page. I have this page in a table format in which I can see which class my tasks are for, when they are due, and any notes that I may have on the assignments.
      • The great thing about Notion is that you can also display tables in various “views”. This means that you can use this table to view all your tasks at once or to filter what you see to a specific class.
    • Class Pages - These are pages that I have created for each of the classes that I am taking this semester. Inside each specific class page, I put my instructor contact information, specific notes for each course, links to relevant websites, and a table for lecture notes. Here are some tips for using Notion for your class notes:
      • Use toggles instead of bullet points when taking notes that you may want to create flashcards for. You can simply use toggles to display and hide information in a similar way to flashcards.
      • If you are taking a course involving code, use code blocks to have your code automatically styled as if you were working in a programming editor.
  • Journal - For this page, I created a column to hold my recent journal entries and a column listing archive pages for each month.

Aside from these pages, I have also created pages for any side projects that I want to pursue and just any thoughts that I may have. You can use Notion for anything you want. The ability to design your pages in any way you want is what makes Notion stand apart from other planning and note-taking platforms. It truly is the all-in-one-workspace!

If you want to get started with Notion and you are a college student, you can sign up as a student with your student email and get the Personal Pro plan for free! Here are some YouTube videos detailing more ways to set up Notion:

Google Calendar - Web, iOS, Android

I’m sure everyone has heard of Google Calendar but I just wanted to include this app in this article since it is an integral part of my workflow. The only apps I use to schedule and keep track of my tasks are Notion and Google Calendar. Google Calendar is a fantastic app that keeps a record of upcoming events and helps plan out your day. It’s also visually simple and appealing to use.

I typically put my Notion and Google Calendar windows side by side and plan out my day, blocking out times for different tasks, meals, and workouts. By doing this either the night before or the morning of, you can keep yourself on track and always know what you need to be doing. I’ve created separate calendars with different colors for my classes, assessments, career, office hours, personal, RSOs, social, and work. I have found that using this method of calendar blocking helps reduce the amount of time I spend wondering what I need to do during the day. Here is a great video on calendar blocking: “CALENDAR BLOCKING // Time Management for Students” - Mariana’s Study Corner.

GoodNotes - iOS/Mac

GoodNotes is an app that I have been using for high school and now for college. If you have an iPad with an Apple Pencil this app is incredibly useful for taking class notes. You can create notebooks and quick notes and categorize them into folders for organization. I love how simple and easy-to-use the interface is. Here are some of my favorite features of GoodNotes:

  • Photo Importing - GoodNotes makes importing photos super smooth because you’re able to preview all your recently taken photos in the toolbar. It’s a super-fast 2-tap process when you are taking notes and doesn’t slow you down.
  • Document Importing - This semester I am taking 2 math courses and my professors have provided fill-in PDF worksheets to use during the lectures. I love how I’m able to AirDrop my downloaded PDF worksheets to my iPad and import them inside my math notebooks.
  • Search Through Notes - The search tool is particularly useful when studying and wanting to immediately find a certain topic in your notes. Being able to search not only through typed text but also handwritten text can be helpful when grinding before a quiz or exam.

I hope my dive into the various ways I try to maintain productivity and efficiency and the apps that I mentioned will help you with managing your time, tasks, and life. Even though working hard is an amazing feeling, never forget to separate your self-worth from your work and allow yourself time to rest. To enjoy life is neither selfish nor unproductive, but the whole point of living life.
